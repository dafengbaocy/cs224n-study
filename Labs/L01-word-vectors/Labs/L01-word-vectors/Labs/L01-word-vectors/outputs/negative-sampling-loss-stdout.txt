======================================================================
CS224N L01 Code Capsule: Negative Sampling Loss vs Full Softmax
======================================================================

Setup:
  Vocabulary size |V| = 10000
  Embedding dimension = 50
  Center word index = 42
  Context word index = 100
  ||v_c|| = 0.7168
  ||u_o|| = 0.7885
  u_o^T v_c = -0.1988

======================================================================
Part 1: Full Softmax Loss (Notes Eq.14)
======================================================================

Eq.14: P(c|o) = exp(u_c^T v_o) / Σ_w exp(u_w^T v_o)
The denominator (partition function) sums over ALL |V| words.

  Score for observed context word (u_o^T v_c): -0.198812
  Partition function Z = Σ_w exp(u_w^T v_c): 7541.361284
  P(context|center) under softmax: 0.00008174
  Softmax loss = -log P(c|o): 9.411909
  Number of exp() evaluations: 10000 (entire vocabulary!)

  Gradient ∂L/∂u_o (softmax):
    = (P(c|o) - 1) · v_c
    = (0.00008174 - 1) · v_c
    ||∂L/∂u_o|| = 0.716708
    NOTE: Computing this gradient required ALL 10000 probabilities!

======================================================================
Part 2: Negative Sampling Loss (Notes Eq.15)
======================================================================

Eq.15: J_NS = -log σ(u_o^T v_c) - Σ_{ℓ=1}^{k} log σ(-u_ℓ^T v_c)
Only k+1 dot products needed instead of |V|!

   k |    NS Loss |  Softmax Loss |     Diff |  Dot products
------------------------------------------------------------
   — |          — |      9.411909 |        — |         10000
   1 |   1.453606 |      9.411909 |  -7.9583 |             2
   5 |   4.299113 |      9.411909 |  -5.1128 |             6
  10 |   7.699720 |      9.411909 |  -1.7122 |            11
  25 |  18.354336 |      9.411909 |  +8.9424 |            26

  Key insight: NS loss does NOT equal softmax loss.
  NS is a DIFFERENT objective — binary classification:
    'Is (center, context) a real pair?' → label 1
    'Is (center, noise_ℓ) a real pair?' → label 0

======================================================================
Part 3: Gradient Direction Comparison
======================================================================

  For the POSITIVE sample term:
  Softmax: ∂L/∂u_o includes (P(c|o) - 1) · v_c
  NS:      ∂L/∂u_o = (σ(u_o^T v_c) - 1) · v_c
  The positive term gradient is IDENTICAL in form!
  The difference: softmax updates ALL other u_w; NS only updates k negatives.

Part 5: Computational Efficiency (gradient computation)
======================================================================

  Softmax gradient: O(|V| × d) — must update ALL output vectors.
  NS gradient: O(k × d) — only k+1 vectors updated.

         |V| |   Softmax grad |  NS grad (k=5) |  Speedup
  -------------------------------------------------------
        1000 |       0.082 ms |       0.055 ms |    1.5x
       10000 |       2.592 ms |       0.176 ms |   14.7x
       50000 |       4.412 ms |       0.840 ms |    5.2x
      100000 |      12.538 ms |       1.586 ms |    7.9x

  At |V|=100K: softmax gradient is ~8x slower than NS(k=5).

Summary
======================================================================

1. Full softmax loss = 9.411909
   - Requires computing exp() for ALL 10000 words
   - Partition function Z = 7541.361284

2. Negative sampling loss (k=5):
   - Only needs k+1 = 6 dot products
   - Loss = binary classification: real pair vs noise

3. Gradient speedup at |V|=100K: softmax 12.538ms vs NS(k=5) 1.586ms
   = 7.9x faster per gradient step

4. Why logistic approximation works:
   - Positive sample gradient has SAME form: (σ(score) - 1) · v_c
   - Negative samples push noise away: (1 - σ(-score)) · u_ℓ
   - "If we randomly push down a few words at each step,
     on average, things will work out." — Notes §3.5

5. Practical k: Mikolov et al. (2013) recommend k=5-20
